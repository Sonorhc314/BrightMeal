# Usability Test Report

## Details

- **Conducted by:** Rafeef Kurdi
- **Date:** 15/04/2026
- **Participant:** Technical consultant, no prior experience with food donation apps
- **Role tested:** Donor
- **Device:** Lenovo Yoga (laptop, browser)
- **Duration:** ~10 minutes
- **Consent:** Participant gave verbal consent to the test being documented.

## Method

**Think-aloud protocol:** The participant was asked to say what they were thinking as they went through each task: what they were looking at, what they expected, and where they got confused. I only stepped in if they got completely stuck.

## Pre-Test Questions

1. Have you used any food donation, delivery, or volunteering apps before? No
2. What would you expect an app like BrightMeal to let you do? List what food I have, quantity, expiry dates, pickup window, have driver's contact details to communicate, keep track of quantities/meals given away.

## Tasks

1. Sign up and create a profile
2. Post a new donation with allergens and a pickup window
3. Check the leaderboard and badge progress
4. View donation history

## Observations

| # | Task | Completed? | Difficulty (1-5) | Time | Think-Aloud Notes |
|---|------|-----------|-----------------|------|-------------------|
| 1 | Create donor profile | Yes | 1 | ~1 min | Suggested adding password verification or show/hide password toggle. |
| 2 | Post a donation | Yes | 1 | ~3 min | Good instant confirmation and being able to always see the details. |
| 3 | Check leaderboard | Yes | 1 | ~1 min | Very nice interface, easy to use and motivating. |
| 4 | View history | Yes | 1 | ~1 min | Nice to see the timeline. |

## Direct Quotes

- "This is a good idea and it aligns really well with the second, twelfth, and seventeenth UN Sustainable Development Goals — 'zero hunger', 'responsible consumption and production', and 'partnerships for the goals'."
- "It's a better version of the app TooGoodToGo as people in need get access to good quality food rather than just eating canned ultraprocessed foods."
- "The app is easy to use even for junior staff or trainees so it's good that we don't have to do extra training for it."

## Post-Test Questions

1. How easy was the app to use overall? (1 = very difficult, 5 = very easy) **5**
2. What was the most confusing part? The difference between "boxed" and "containers" in storage handling, which should be clearer. She also questioned why the donor chooses the pickup method rather than letting the charity decide, and suggested a separate field for postcode.
3. What did you like the most? The allergens section. She liked the "None" option because you have to select allergens and can't skip, and when you choose "None" you can't select anything else. She also really liked the timing option.
4. Would you use an app like this? Why or why not? Yes, it's nice and good motivation because it saves food from dumpsters while also helping people who need meals. The app is easy to use, straightforward, and has a really nice UI.

## Issues Found

1. **Low**: Difference between "boxed" and "containers" in storage handling is unclear to the participant.
2. **Low**: Participant questioned why the donor chooses the delivery method (driver vs charity pickup) rather than the charity deciding.
3. **Low**: Participant suggested a separate postcode field for pickup location.

## Suggestions from Participant

- Since the acceptance window isn't instant, a restaurant might list food before closing and still sell some of it the normal way before a charity picks it up. Could the quantities be editable until the offer is accepted?
- Cost of extra packaging if meals are packaged individually. If food is given in a large container, does the donor get it back?
- Allow users to download a badge/leaderboard image (JPG or PDF) to post on social media.
- Add password verification or show/hide toggle on signup.

## Reflections

Really useful session. I got feedback on things I wouldn't have noticed as a developer but that clearly mattered from a donor's point of view.
