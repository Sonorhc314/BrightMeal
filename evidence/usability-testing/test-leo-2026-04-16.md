# Usability Test Report

## Details

- **Conducted by:** Leonid Shevchenko
- **Date:** 16/04/2026
- **Participant:** 3rd year Politics student, never used a food donation app
- **Role tested:** Charity
- **Device:** Chrome on laptop
- **Duration:** ~8 minutes
- **Consent:** Participant gave verbal consent to the test being documented.

## Method

**Think-aloud protocol:** The participant was asked to say what they were thinking as they went through each task: what they were looking at, what they expected, and anywhere they got confused. I only stepped in when they were completely stuck.

## Pre-Test Questions

1. Have you used any food donation, delivery, or volunteering apps before? No, has done some volunteering but never with an app.
2. What would you expect an app like BrightMeal to let you do? "For a charity, I assume it will let me accept donations. So it has to show some information about food being donated — quantity, quality, who is donating. Also when to expect donations you accepted. Maybe a way to talk to the driver who is delivering it."

## Tasks

1. Sign up and create a charity profile
2. Browse available offers and accept one
3. Check the leaderboard and badge progress
4. View donation history

## Observations

| # | Task | Completed? | Difficulty (1-5) | Time | Think-Aloud Notes |
|---|------|-----------|-----------------|------|-------------------|
| 1 | Create charity profile | Yes | 1 | ~30s | Noticed email validation let through `blobloblb@test` without a real TLD. No confirmation emails. Questioned why the address field is just the city. Asked whether a charity should have to register with a government charity registration number. |
| 2 | Browse offers and accept | Yes | 2 | ~5m | Questioned allergen accuracy on some offers. Confused by star ratings, unsure how they were derived. Noted "containers" vs "boxed" felt like the same thing. Saw offers whose latest collection date had passed. Searched "chicken" and did not get "buffalo wings" back. Wasn't sure whether the decline button hid offers globally or just for them; tested it, and noted the offer didn't disappear at all. Eventually accepted a Victoria sponge successfully. |
| 3 | Check leaderboard & badges | Yes | 1 | ~1m | Charity showed zero impact despite having just accepted an offer, which the participant put down to delivery not being complete yet. Said there was no clear differentiation between "saved by me" and "saved overall". Said points were unexplained ("can I spend them on merch or what?"). Couldn't locate the badges view. |
| 4 | View history | Yes | 1 | ~10s | History only shows completed orders, so nothing visible initially. Facilitator marked the test delivery complete for them, after which the donation appeared with notifications and updated impact. Noted the per-donation call button "doesn't seem to do anything". |

## Direct Quotes

- "Some of [the offers] didn't have correct allergens marked I think??"
- "Shouldn't the charity be registering with a government charity registration number?"
- "Why can I see these donation offers if the latest collection date has passed?"
- "No idea what those points mean, and why would I want them? Like can I spend them on merch or something?"
- "Overall is nice and easy to use, but obviously needs way more polish before production. Even if it's only for the trusted participants."
- "I guess it's okay for a prototype."

## Post-Test Questions

1. How easy was the app to use overall? (1 = very difficult, 5 = very easy) **2**: "very easy, although I couldn't find the badges. The core functionality seems to be there." (Comment was largely positive; the low numeric score appears driven by the badge-discovery and polish issues.)
2. What was the most confusing part? "No badges…". Couldn't find where badges live.
3. What did you like the most? "A simple interface, branding seems nice, all the colours are pretty."
4. Would you use an app like this? Why or why not? "When it's fully finished — sure, but maybe not for now."

## Issues Found

1. **High**: Weak sign-up validation: allowed registration with malformed email (`blobloblb@test`) and no confirmation emails or email-verification flow.
2. **High**: No charity verification mechanism (e.g. Charity Commission number), so legitimacy can't be confirmed at onboarding.
3. **High**: Inaccurate allergen information on some existing offers; food-safety-critical given Natasha's Law requirements.
4. **High**: Expired offers (past latest collection date) remain visible in the charity browse view.
5. **Medium**: Star rating on donor profiles shown without explanation of how it's derived (it's the FSA Food Hygiene Rating, but this isn't labelled).
6. **Medium**: Search doesn't match related terms (e.g. "chicken" doesn't return "buffalo wings"), it's purely substring-based.
7. **Medium**: "Decline" action has unclear and apparently broken behaviour: user expected the offer to disappear for them and it did not.
8. **Medium**: Impact metrics on leaderboard don't update until delivery completes, with no inline explanation of pending vs confirmed state.
9. **Medium**: Points system lacks a value proposition. No explanation of what points unlock or why a charity would care about them.
10. **Medium**: Call button in history / detail views is non-functional (no `tel:` link or action attached).
11. **Low**: Address field at sign-up only accepts a city, not a full address or postcode.
12. **Low**: Packaging options "containers" and "boxed" feel duplicative and are not clearly differentiated.
13. **Low**: Badges view is hard to locate from the charity navigation.
14. **Low**: Weak visual hierarchy between "saved by me" and "saved overall" sections on the leaderboard/profile.
15. **Low**: Participant summary: "needs way more polish before production". General UX refinement.

## Suggestions from Participant

- Add proper email validation and email confirmation at sign-up.
- Require a government charity registration number when a charity onboards.
- Explain the rating system inline (what it is, how it's calculated).
- Improve search to handle related terms (tagging or fuzzy/keyword expansion).
- Clarify the "decline" action so it visibly removes the offer from the user's view.
- Make impact metrics clearer by showing an "accepted (pending)" state separate from confirmed impact.
- Strengthen the visual difference between "saved by me" and "saved overall".
- Explain the value of points, either by linking them to rewards or clearly framing them as a gamification signal.
- Make the badges view more discoverable from the main navigation.
- Make the call button functional (e.g. `tel:` link to the donor / driver phone).
- General polish pass before production.

## Reflections

Plenty of polish still needed, but the main thing is the participant got through all four tasks without help, so the charity flow works. The two issues I care about most are on the food-safety side (wrong allergens on some demo offers, expired offers still showing up) and the trust side (no charity verification at sign-up, weak email validation). The charity interviews raised the same trust point independently, so a Charity Commission lookup at sign-up feels like the obvious next step. The confusion around the star rating and the points system was similar to what Rafeef picked up on with the "boxed" vs "containers" labels, and makes me think the labels and terminology need a separate cleanup pass. A few of the things the participant hit on (broken call button, decline button doing nothing, badges being hard to find) are just bugs, and we should be upfront about them rather than try to explain them away.
