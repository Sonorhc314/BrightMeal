# Usability Test Report

## Details

- **Conducted by:** Riad Mehyar
- **Date:** 10/04/2026
- **Participant:** 4th year Engineering student, no prior experience as a driver or with food donation apps
- **Role tested:** Driver
- **Device:** iPhone 16 Pro (mobile, browser)
- **Duration:** ~10 minutes
- **Consent:** Participant gave verbal consent to the test being documented.

## Method

**Think-aloud protocol:** The participant was asked to say what he was thinking as he went through each task: what he was looking at, what he expected, and where he got confused. I only stepped in if he got completely stuck.

## Pre-Test Questions

1. Have you used any food donation, delivery, or volunteering apps before? Nothing really beforehand. I use Deliveroo quite often however never being the driver. I also never volunteered as a driver before.
2. What would you expect an app like BrightMeal to let you do? Something involving picking up food that restaurants don't want and taking it to another place, similar to Too Good To Go but with a twist.

## Tasks

1. Sign up and create a profile
2. View available jobs and accept one
3. Mark as picked up, then delivered
4. Check the leaderboard and badge progress
5. View delivery history

## Observations

| # | Task | Completed? | Difficulty (1-5) | Time | Think-Aloud Notes |
|---|------|-----------|-----------------|------|-------------------|
| 1 | Create driver profile | Yes | 1 | ~1 min | Completed without hesitation. Said "this is very standard and easy." |
| 2 | View available jobs and accept one | Yes | 2 | ~1.5 min | Viewed available jobs and immediately chose most preferred one. Said "very clear and great that it shows location of donor before accepting, makes it very convenient." |
| 3 | Mark as picked up, then delivered | Yes | 2 | ~2 min | Used Navigate to Pickup button immediately. Followed the timeline. Found "Mark as Delivered" button without any prompting. Said "very satisfying and well structured." |
| 4 | Check leaderboard and rank | Yes | 1 | ~30 sec | Found Rank in the nav immediately. Looked at the points total and said "what do I spend these on?" Did not explore further unprompted. |
| 5 | View delivery history | Yes | 1 | ~30 sec | Tapped History in the nav. Scanned past jobs. Said "I like seeing the timeline for each one." Pretty straightforward. |

## Direct Quotes

- "What do I spend or how to use the points from the rank? Is it a reward?"
- "Satisfying flow and very well structured software."
- "I think starting as non-profit is great but you need a proper business plan to grow across the UK and make profit."

## Post-Test Questions

1. How easy was the app to use overall? (1 = very difficult, 5 = very easy) **4**
2. What was the most confusing part? Nothing really, but if I had to pick something it would be the rank or point system. I thought it was something redeemable but after you explained it, it is clear. A driver joins this movement purely as a volunteering incentive which makes sense.
3. What did you like the most? The timeline of events. It's clear and straightforward, not too complex and not too basic, properly structured.
4. Would you use an app like this? Why or why not? Yes probably, it all depends on how free my schedule is and how close the jobs are. Since it is a local idea then I think it is definitely a motivating factor since Bath is quite small and easy to move around.

## Issues Found

1. **High**: Point system has no explanation. The points figure was displayed with no indication of what they represent or how much is earned per delivery. Some confusion was caused.
2. **Medium**: The status label "Accepted" is confusing for drivers. Does it mean the job has already been taken by another driver? Needs a clearer label like "Charity Confirmed, Driver Needed."
3. **Low**: Push notifications are needed. A driver will not open the app randomly; it would be better to send a notification when a new job is available for better regular engagement.

## Suggestions from Participant

- Make the points system redeemable so it is an even more driving factor for drivers. Perhaps working on sponsors and finding ways to generate monthly revenue.
- Push notifications for new available jobs near the driver's location.

## Reflections

Quick and clear test. The driver flow is straightforward and the timeline is good. All the tasks were easy and the whole thing took under 10 minutes with barely any hesitation. The only real point of confusion was the points system, which was easy to clear up once I explained it. Beyond that the feedback was minor. He also made a broader point about the business side, suggesting we build out more than one revenue stream as the app scales so it can start generating profit.
